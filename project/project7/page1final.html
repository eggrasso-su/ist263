Exploring Fashion Through Self-Expression

My Fashion Journey: From Childhood to Corporate Life

My Fashion Journey - From Childhood to Today

Ever since I was young, fashion has been one of my passions. When I was in elementary school, my role model in fashion was my mom. She always encouraged me to dress myself before I could even go to school and gave me full creative authority. My mom inspired me to prioritize what I felt comfortable in and the styles that I was drawn to rather than wearing what everyone else in my grade was wearing. This sense of freedom taught me that fashion is about feeling good in your own skin.

Fashion, for me, has always been a creative outlet. It has allowed me to express myself uniquely, which has been a powerful source of confidence. Whether I was layering dresses over jeans as a kid or mixing patterns in my teen years, I loved that I could showcase my personality through what I wore.

How Fashion Led Me to a Professional Career

As Ive grown up, my passion for fashion has evolved from a personal interest to something that also plays a big role in my professional life. Shopping and styling myself, as well as my friends and family, have become significant hobbies of mine. I love being able to share my style advice and seeing how others incorporate it into their unique looks.

This past summer, I was fortunate enough to take my passion into the corporate world as a buying intern at Burlington Stores in New York City. This was a pivotal moment where I was able to channel the confidence and self-expression that fashion had given me into something impactful for others. I felt a deep connection to the work because I knew that each item I chose could help someone else feel the same sense of confidence and joy that fashion has given me over the years.

Being in this role also made me realize how fashion influences our daily lives. It's not just about wearing clothes; it's about making a statement and giving others the opportunity to express themselves as well. This experience made me even more excited about the impact I can have in the fashion industry.

The Ever-Evolving World of Fashion Trends

One of the most interesting aspects of fashion to me is how it constantly repeats and evolves. Styles that my mom wore when she was growing up are becoming trendy again today. Fashion has this unique way of cycling through old trends with new twists that reflect our current preferences and lifestyles.

A few key examples of these recurring trends are “mom” jeans and chunky sneakers—items that were extremely popular decades ago, declined in popularity, and are now staples for trendsetters. It’s fascinating to watch these pieces return with a fresh perspective and become beloved by a new generation.

There are several prominent trends that have dominated the fashion scene recently, and they each have their own distinctive appeal:

  • Minimalism: This trend focuses on simplicity and prioritizes basics rather than chasing after micro-trends. It's a direct response to the impact of fast fashion and aims to embrace timeless, versatile pieces.
  • Maximalism: A counter-trend to minimalism, maximalism celebrates the abstract, bold, and excessive sides of fashion. This style is all about bright colors, clashing patterns, and an over-the-top aesthetic that encourages self-expression.
  • Streetwear: Originally emerging from the hip-hop, surfing, and skateboarding communities, streetwear combines comfort with style. It includes items such as sneakers, hoodies, and graphic T-shirts and has become a major influence on both high fashion and everyday style.

Understanding these trends is important because it reveals how fashion reflects and reacts to cultural movements. Whether it's embracing minimalism in reaction to environmental concerns or celebrating maximalism as a form of artistic expression, trends are a direct reflection of the world around us.
